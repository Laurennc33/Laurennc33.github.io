What is Formula One?

Formula One, often abbreviated as F1, is an international motorsport featuring the fastest open-wheel cars in the world. Each season consists of a series of races, known as Grand Prix, held in different countries around the globe. What makes F1 unique is the combination of cutting-edge technology, highly skilled drivers, and strategic teamwork. Teams like Ferrari, Mercedes, and Red Bull compete not only to win races but to develop the best cars and innovations.

  • Each race weekend has three key stages: practice, qualifying, and the race.
  • Drivers must balance speed and strategy to maximize tire performance and fuel efficiency.
  • The championship consists of two titles: the Driver's Championship and the Constructor's Championship.
